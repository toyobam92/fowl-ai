How to use this template

What makes an AI platform resume different

A resume for AI platform work is not the same as a traditional job resume. You are not applying to a hiring manager who reads between the lines — you are being processed by an AI matching system that is looking for specific signals.

Three things matter more than everything else:

  • Domain specificity — Your industry and function must be named explicitly. "Data" scores lower than "financial services predictive modeling."
  • Tool and technology keywords — AI matching systems look for named technologies. List every relevant tool, language, and framework you actually use.
  • An AI Work section — A dedicated section showing any AI-adjacent experience, even if it is from your regular job. This is the highest-signal addition most applicants miss entirely.
The key difference

Traditional resumes show career progression. AI platform resumes show judgment capability. Reframe every bullet point around: what did you evaluate, what did you catch, what did you improve, what did you decide?

Section Guide

What to write in each section — and why it matters

The Summary

This is the most important section for AI platform matching. It needs: domain, years, industry, tools, and your AI evaluation capability — all in 2–3 sentences. Nothing vague.

Weak

"Experienced data professional passionate about AI and machine learning with strong analytical skills."

Strong

"Data scientist with 6 years in financial services predictive modeling. Proficient in Python and SQL. Currently evaluating AI outputs on Mercor at $75/hr in data science and ML domains."

The AI Evaluation Section

This is the section most applicants miss. Even if you have never worked on an AI platform before, you almost certainly have AI-adjacent experience from your regular job. Reviewing model outputs, evaluating analytical work, or even using AI tools professionally all count — frame them as evaluation experience.

If you have no platform experience yet

Write: "[Current company] — Review and validate [type of output] for [quality standard]. Evaluate [type of work] produced by [colleagues/AI tools] for correctness and accuracy." This is real experience that platforms can use.

Experience bullet points

Weak bullet

"Worked on machine learning models for the analytics team."

Strong bullet

"Built uplift models in Python (XGBoost) for retention campaigns, improving ROI by 23% across 4M cardholders."

Skills section — keywords that matter

AI platform matching systems scan for specific technologies. Always include every language, library, and framework you actually use. Do not group them vaguely as "programming languages" — list each one individually as a tag.

Final checklist before submitting

  • Summary includes the word "RLHF" and names your specific domain, industry, and top tools — no vague phrases
  • AI Evaluation section is present with at least 2–3 specific bullets
  • Every job bullet leads with a high-signal action verb — use these: Evaluated · Reviewed · Identified · Assessed · Built · Calibrated · Validated · Annotated · Benchmarked · Analyzed
  • At least one bullet per role includes a measurable outcome (%, $, volume, timeframe)
  • Skills section lists every technology individually — no grouped categories. Include at least 3 universal AI keywords (RLHF, LLM evaluation, GenAI, human feedback, model alignment)
  • LinkedIn URL is included — and your LinkedIn headline exactly matches your resume title. Mercor cross-checks LinkedIn during screening; a mismatch lowers your match score
  • "GenAI" or "LLM" appears somewhere in the summary or AI Evaluation section — stronger signal than just "AI"
Keep updating it

As you complete work on AI platforms, add it to your AI Evaluation section with the platform name, type of work, and rate. Each entry makes your profile more credible and your matching score higher.

Bonus — Platform Profile Template

Your platform bio: what to write during onboarding

Every AI platform asks you to describe yourself during onboarding. This is not the same as your resume — it is a shorter, keyword-dense description that the platform's matching system reads directly. Most people write it as an afterthought. It is actually one of the most important things you will write, because it determines which task categories you are offered and at what rate.

The formula

[Years] + [Domain] + [Industry] + [Specific tools] + [What you evaluate/judge] + [RLHF availability]
Every field. No vague words. Nothing generic.

Blank profile bio template

[X]-year [job title] with deep expertise in [specific function: e.g., predictive modeling / financial analysis / software architecture] across [industry: e.g., fintech / healthcare / legal / energy]. Proficient in [Tool 1], [Tool 2], [Tool 3], and [Tool 4]. Experienced in [specific type of evaluation or judgment work: e.g., reviewing model outputs for bias and correctness / evaluating code logic and efficiency / assessing statistical reasoning quality]. Available for RLHF, AI output evaluation, and domain expert annotation requiring [domain] judgment.

Completed example — Data Scientist

10-year data scientist with deep expertise in predictive modeling, uplift modeling, and causal inference across fintech and payments. Proficient in Python, SQL (Presto/Trino), Spark, XGBoost, and scikit-learn. Experienced in reviewing ML model outputs for statistical correctness, evaluating AI-generated code for bugs and inefficiencies, and assessing reasoning quality in quantitative analysis. Available for RLHF, AI output evaluation, and domain expert annotation requiring data science and financial modeling judgment.

Completed example — Software Engineer

8-year software engineer specializing in backend systems and API architecture across SaaS and enterprise products. Proficient in Python, TypeScript, Go, PostgreSQL, and AWS. Experienced in reviewing AI-generated code for correctness, security vulnerabilities, and production viability. Strong in evaluating algorithm design, system architecture recommendations, and debugging explanations produced by LLMs. Available for RLHF, code evaluation, and technical AI output review.

Completed example — Finance / Analyst

7-year finance professional with expertise in financial modeling, equity research, and credit risk analysis across investment banking and corporate finance. Proficient in Excel, Python, SQL, and Bloomberg. Experienced in evaluating AI-generated financial analyses for methodological correctness, identifying errors in valuation logic, and assessing the quality of quantitative reasoning. Available for RLHF and domain expert evaluation requiring finance, accounting, and investment judgment.

Platform-specific notes

Mercor: This bio text should match your LinkedIn headline exactly. Mercor cross-checks.
Outlier: List every skill domain separately — "data science" AND "Python" AND "statistics" score independently.
Handshake AI: Add your education level explicitly — "M.S. Data Science" or "B.S. Computer Science." Fellowship track assignment uses this.
Surge AI: Lead with your academic credentials if you have a PhD or MD. This is the single most important signal for Surge's matching system.
