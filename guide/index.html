Chapter 01

The AI income landscape right now

AI companies are in a race to make their models better. To do that, they need humans to do three things: generate training data, evaluate model outputs, and catch errors that AI cannot catch itself.

This has created an entirely new category of work. It is not traditional freelancing. It is not a 9-to-5. It sits in between — flexible, remote, and increasingly well-paid for people with the right background.

The three tiers of AI income work

Tier 1 — High judgment, high pay ($50–$150/hr): Platforms like Mercor and Invisible AI that need genuine domain expertise. You are not clicking labels — you are evaluating whether an AI's reasoning is correct in your field. Entry is harder but the rates reflect it.

Tier 2 — Skilled evaluation ($20–$50/hr): Platforms like Outlier AI and DataAnnotation where you assess AI outputs across writing, coding, and reasoning tasks. Good starting point if you have a strong background in any knowledge domain.

Tier 3 — Volume annotation ($8–$20/hr): Platforms like Appen and Remotasks. Lower barrier to entry, lower pay. Useful for getting started and building a track record, but not where you want to stay.

The key insight

The difference between Tier 1 and Tier 3 is not how hard you work — it is how you position your existing expertise. This guide shows you how to move up.

Mercor: the Tier 1 playbook

Mercor
Top Pay Hardest Entry
Pay: $70–$150/hr
Type: AI Evaluation & Technical Projects
Best for: Engineers, data scientists, domain experts

Mercor uses AI to match technical talent to high-value projects. The interview process is more rigorous than most platforms — but that is exactly why it pays more. Most people who get rejected do so because they treat it like a job application instead of a technical screening.

How the Mercor interview works

  1. AI screening call — Mercor's AI interviews you via video. It asks about your background, experience, and technical skills. Speak clearly, be specific, and name real tools and frameworks you have used.
  2. Technical assessment — Depending on your domain, you will receive a task to complete. For data roles, expect SQL, Python, or ML problem-solving. For other domains, expect domain-specific reasoning tasks.
  3. Project matching — Once cleared, Mercor matches you to active projects. Response time matters — projects fill quickly.

What the AI screening actually wants to hear

The AI is calibrating your level. Use specific language: mention real projects, real outcomes, real tools. Vague answers ("I have experience in data analysis") score lower than specific ones ("I built uplift models in Python using scikit-learn to optimize credit card retention campaigns").

Tip that works

Before your Mercor screening, write out 3 specific projects you have worked on: what the problem was, what tools you used, and what the measurable outcome was. Use that language in the interview. Specificity is what separates $70/hr candidates from rejected ones.

Outlier AI & DataAnnotation: the Tier 2 entry points

Outlier AI
Best Entry Point
Pay: $20–$50/hr
Best for: Writers, coders, researchers, STEM professionals

Outlier is the most accessible Tier 2 platform and a strong starting point. Work is flexible — you pick up tasks when available. The most valuable tasks involve rating AI-generated code, evaluating creative writing, and answering domain-specific questions.

Getting the best tasks on Outlier

Outlier segments its workforce by expertise. When you sign up, list your strongest domains prominently — data science, finance, medicine, law, engineering. The higher-value tasks (coding review, STEM evaluation) are only offered to profiles that signal relevant expertise.

Important

On Outlier, your rating score determines future task access. Take the first few tasks slowly. A strong early score unlocks higher-paying task categories. Speed matters less than accuracy in the beginning.

DataAnnotation
Best for Developers
Pay: $20–$45/hr
Best for: Software engineers, data professionals

DataAnnotation focuses heavily on RLHF tasks for code. If you write or review code professionally, this platform pays well and provides steady work. The tasks are mentally engaging compared to standard annotation.

How to maximize earnings on DataAnnotation

Focus on the coding and reasoning tasks — they pay significantly more than the basic annotation tasks. Indicate your programming languages and technical background clearly during onboarding. Python, SQL, and JavaScript specialists consistently access higher-value task queues.

When to push back on a rate

If a platform's offered rate is significantly below your benchmark, two things are happening: either your profile is not signalling your expertise clearly enough, or the platform is not the right fit for your background. Adjust your profile language before accepting a lower rate — it is often a positioning problem, not a skills problem.

Positioning tip

Your rate on AI platforms is directly tied to how specifically you describe your domain expertise during onboarding. "Data professional" earns less than "data scientist with 5 years in fintech predictive modeling." Be specific. Always.

Your first 30 days: the exact action plan

Week 1 — Apply and position

  1. Write your expertise statement — One paragraph that describes your domain, your tools, and your measurable outcomes. This is what you will use on every platform profile.
  2. Apply to Mercor — Use your expertise statement. Be specific in the AI screening call. Do not rush.
  3. Apply to Outlier AI — Sign up, complete the skills assessment, and list all relevant domains.
  4. Apply to DataAnnotation — Apply if you have a coding or technical background.

Week 2 — Start earning while waiting

  1. Start on Appen or Remotasks — Begin building baseline income while Tier 1 and 2 approvals process.
  2. Complete Outlier onboarding tasks — Focus on accuracy, not speed. Build your score.
  3. Follow up on Mercor — If no response after 7 days, reapply with a refined profile.

Week 3 — Optimize and stack

  1. Identify your highest-paying active platform — Allocate 70% of your available hours here.
  2. Apply to Invisible AI — If you have senior domain expertise, add this to your pipeline.
  3. Track hours and income — Know your effective hourly rate across all platforms.

Week 4 — Scale what works

  1. Double down on your top 2 platforms — Drop any platform paying below your average rate.
  2. Increase availability on your best platform — More hours on the highest-paying source.
  3. Review and adjust your profile — Update language based on what tasks you are being matched to.

The mistakes that slow most people down

Mistake 1

Being vague on your profile. "Experience in data" earns less than "5 years building ML pipelines in Python for financial services." Specificity is your rate multiplier.

Mistake 2

Anchoring on one platform. Approvals take time. Apply to 3–4 simultaneously so income starts as soon as possible instead of weeks later.

Mistake 3

Prioritizing speed over quality on early tasks. Your rating on Outlier and DataAnnotation determines future access. A low score on your first 10 tasks can lock you out of higher-paying work permanently.

Mistake 4

Staying on Tier 3 platforms too long. Appen and Remotasks are entry points, not destinations. Move up as soon as Tier 2 approvals arrive.

Mistake 5

Not tracking income by platform. Without tracking, you cannot see which platforms deserve more of your time. A simple spreadsheet with date, platform, hours, and payout is enough.

Final word

The AI income opportunity is real and it is now. The platforms are hiring, the rates are strong for the right profiles, and the barrier is not technical skill — it is positioning. Know your expertise, state it clearly, apply to the right platforms, and protect your rating in the early weeks.
